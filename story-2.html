“One Street, One Cleanup”

Community

I sent out a message in the neighborhood group chat: “Meet at the corner at 10. Bring gloves if you have them.”

Five people showed up, that was it. And honestly, that was enough. We picked up what we could, wrappers in the grass, bottles near the drain, the pile of plastic that's always just there.

Halfway through, a neighbor walked over and asked, “Do you need more bags?” Then two kids joined, competing to see who could fill a bag the fastest with trash. No one wanted a reward, people just wanted the street to feel like home.

The best part wasn’t the end result. It was what happened after. The next week, someone asked, “Want to do it again?”
